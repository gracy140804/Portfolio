Project Abstract

Telehealth has expanded remote access to healthcare, allowing patients to connect with providers from anywhere. However, current systems still fall short in understanding a patient’s emotional state during virtual interactions. They also lack intelligent mechanisms to recommend the most suitable doctor. This creates gaps in personalization and holistic care delivery. To overcome these limitations, this project introduces an AI-driven telehealth system capable of analyzing emotional, behavioral, and clinical cues in real time. After the patient logs in, the platform automatically captures facial expressions and interprets emotional states using the Multimodal Emotion Transformer (MET). The system then asks symptom-based questions, and the patient’s spoken responses are processed through the Conformer architecture for highly accurate speech-to-text conversion. The generated text is analyses using Med-BERT, a domain-specific language model designed to extract medical meaning, identify symptoms, and understand the patient’s clinical context. In addition, the system continuously monitors emotional fluctuations throughout the interaction, enabling deeper insights into the patient’s psychological state. The analyzed data is fused to build a comprehensive multimodal profile that represents both emotional and physical indicators. Based on this combined understanding, the system uses Neural Collaborative Filtering (NCF) to deliver highly personalized doctor recommendations. This ensures patients are matched with specialists who best fit their medical requirements and emotional comfort levels. The platform also incorporates secure communication and real-time response assessment for seamless teleconsultations, while its feedback-learning mechanism continuously improves recommendation accuracy. Ultimately, the system elevates remote healthcare by providing a more empathetic, precise, and user-centered telehealth experience.
